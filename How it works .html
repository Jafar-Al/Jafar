About the Project

Our project is an Artificial Intelligence system designed to detect lung cancer by analyzing chest X-ray images. We trained our custom-built Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model from scratch using a dataset of 112,000 images provided by the National Institutes of Health (NIH). All code, from training to deployment, was developed entirely by our team in Python. Initially, we created a basic interface using Python's Tkinter library, and later developed a full-featured web application interface. We used TensorFlow as our main deep learning library.

Note: This system does not replace doctors — it is a tool to assist them in making more accurate diagnoses.

How it Works

  • The system receives a chest X-ray image as input.
  • The image is processed by a CNN model trained to recognize patterns associated with lung cancer.
  • The model outputs a prediction with a high level of accuracy.
  • The result is displayed to the user through an easy-to-use web interface.
  • The system is designed to assist medical professionals in making faster and more reliable diagnoses.

Benefits

  • Fast: Provides results in just a few seconds.
  • Accurate: Achieved high accuracy during testing and validation.
  • Assists Doctors: Helps reduce human error by acting as a second opinion.
  • User-Friendly: Simple web interface suitable for clinical use.
  • Scalable: Can be adapted to work with other types of medical imaging in the future.

AI for Lung Disease Diagnosis

In addition to lung cancer detection, our system is also capable of diagnosing a variety of other lung diseases such as pneumonia and tuberculosis using advanced deep learning techniques. The AI model analyzes chest X-ray images and identifies disease-specific patterns that may not be immediately visible to the human eye.

By expanding the scope of the system, we aim to provide a more comprehensive diagnostic tool that can support doctors in identifying multiple pulmonary conditions efficiently and accurately.
